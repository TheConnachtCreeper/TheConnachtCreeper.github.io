The Muckross Monster

Muckross Lake is the deepest lake in Ireland, which of course makes it ideal for housing a monster. But the Muckross Lake Monster (or “Muckie” for short) is a relatively new find, although with some potentially very old roots.

The story of the Muckie is simple, and also very unusual compared to other cryptids. In 2003, a team of researchers were scanning the lake with sonar looking for a type of critically endangered fish, and their scan showed a large form the size of a two-story house near the bottom of the south-eastern part of the lake.

And that’s it! To my knowledge, no one has seen this monster since the research team discovered it, or if they have, they’re keeping quiet. Let that sink in for a second: we have a cryptid, in a lake that is a major attraction due to the natural beauty of the area, and no one has seen the monster.

Or have they? Maybe no one has seen the monster in recent years, but if you go way back into the local lore, you might be able to find stories of its existence. Apparently, legend has it that the lake is home to an enormous serpent, and one of the nearby mountains is called Cnoc na Peiste, or “Peak of the Serpent”.

But though science has shown that a monster might exist, Muckie has not seen fit to make itself known recently. But that’s no reason not to keep your eyes peeled!
